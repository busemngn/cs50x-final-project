Picture


Adding a picture to your CV can be a cultural and industry-specific choice. In some countries and professions, including a professional headshot is common, while in others, it's not typically expected. Here are some considerations:

When to Include a Picture:
  1. Cultural Norms: In some countries, like many European ones, it's customary to include a professional photo on your CV. It's seen as a way to make the CV more personal.
  2. Client-Facing Roles: If you're applying for roles where appearance is relevant, such as modeling, acting, or customer service, a photo might be beneficial.
When to Avoid Including a Picture:
  1. Anti-Discrimination: In many places, CVs are evaluated based on skills, qualifications, and experiences. Including a photo can introduce the potential for unconscious bias or discrimination.
  2. Professionalism: For most jobs that aren't client-facing or in the entertainment industry, your skills, experiences, and achievements are more relevant than your appearance.

Tips for Including a Picture:

  1. Professional Quality: If you decide to include a picture, ensure it's a high-quality, professional headshot. The background should be neutral, and you should be well-groomed and dressed appropriately.
  2. Positioning: Place the photo at the top of your CV, usually in the header. Ensure it doesn't take up too much space and doesn't distract from the rest of your content.
  3. Consistency: If you include a picture, use the same photo on your other professional profiles like LinkedIn.


!

Before deciding to include a photo on your CV, research the norms in your industry and region. If you're uncertain, it might be a good idea to err on the side of caution and exclude the photo to ensure that your application is evaluated purely on your qualifications and skills.
